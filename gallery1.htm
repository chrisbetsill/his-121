This is an image of floor tiles depicting Richard I spearing Saladin on horseback. The floor tiles are dated to 1250-60 and were made from ceramic. They were found in Chertsey Abbey in Surrey. We accessed information about the tiles from Teaching History with 100 objects from the British Museum. It is significant because it depicts Richard I and Saladin in direct combat with each other, which did not happen. Their armies fought several times, which means the tiles are likely symbolic of the larger conflict between the two.
